Virtual Grocery Assistant

Project Overview

Problem

FreshNest is an AI-powered virtual grocery shopping assistant designed to simplify and personalize the online shopping experience for students and busy professionals.

The app features smart grocery lists, one-tap reordering, price comparisons, and eco-friendly product suggestions. By leveraging user data and AI, it reduces shopping time, improves delivery efficiency, and supports sustainable choices. Our team followed a Lean UX process through research, ideation, prototyping, and user testing to validate the solution.

Students and busy professionals often face frustration with online grocery shopping due to time-consuming item searches, lack of personalized recommendations, inefficient delivery systems, and poor budget management tools. Existing platforms fail to provide smart automation, real-time updates, or sustainable product options—making the overall experience slow, repetitive, and inconvenient.

User Interview

To better understand the needs and pain points of our target users, we conducted 5 in-depth interviews with a mix of students and working professionals aged 22-40. The participants included frequent online shoppers, in-store shoppers, and price-sensitive users.

User Research and Competitive Analysis

To better understand the pain points in online grocery shopping, we conducted interviews with five users including students and working professionals. Key insights revealed that users found the process time-consuming, lacked personalized recommendations, faced delivery delays, and struggled with price comparison and sustainable shopping options. In parallel, we analyzed leading competitors like Instacart, Walmart, FreshDirect, and Amazon Fresh. While these platforms offered convenience and product variety, they failed to deliver a fully personalized, budget-friendly, and eco-conscious experience. These insights guided the development of FreshNest, focusing on AI-driven grocery lists, smart recommendations, price tracking, and sustainable product suggestions.

Persona and Visual Design

The target user is a student or working professional aged 22–29 who prefers online grocery shopping for its convenience and time-saving benefits. They value features like personalized recommendations, reliable stock availability, eco-friendly products, and budget tools like price comparisons. However, they face challenges such as limited time, out-of-stock items, high prices, and difficulty finding the best deals. They use apps like Instacart and Amazon Fresh but seek a more efficient and tailored experience.
